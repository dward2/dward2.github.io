Prelude "Pièce Héroique" César Franck

A note from Tim Baker: This prelude is about good triumphing over evil. The evil is represented by minor keys and sinister melodies that start the music. After a few minutes, there’s an interruption by a quiet march motif, much like the donkey that Jesus rides into Jerusalem. Then we are in a major key with a sweet melody on softer stops. Gradually the minor key’s evil melodies come back and get louder and louder. The piece concludes with full organ playing that sweet major key melody, but this time it sounds utterly triumphant. As Christians, let us feel the meaning and emotions of Jesus’ journey to the cross and then beyond the grave.

*Hymn 278 "Hosanna, Loud Hosanna"

Hosanna, loud hosanna, the little children sang;
through pillared court and temple the lovely anthem rang.
To Jesus, who had blessed them close folded to his breast,
the children sang their praises, the simplest and the best.

From Olivet they followed mid an exultant crowd,
the victor palm branch waving, and chanting clear and loud.
The Lord of earth and heaven rode on in lowly state,
nor scorned that little children should on his bidding wait.

“Hosanna in the highest!” that ancient song we sing,
for Christ is our Redeemer, the Lord of heaven our King.
O may we ever praise him with heart and life and voice,
and in his blissful presence eternally rejoice!
